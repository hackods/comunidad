Who Are We?

A data journey through 150 changemakers

We are 150 people organized in 50 teams of 3, united by one mission: building a more sustainable world.

But numbers alone don’t tell our story. Let’s scroll through who we really are.

150

people · 50 teams · 1 mission

Our Voices

What does our group look like?

68 women (45.3%) form the largest group.

62 men (41.3%) are close behind.

And 20 non-binary people (13.3%) enrich our perspectives.

What Do We Do?

We come from 12 different career fields. Sustainability needs every discipline.

Software Engineers (28) and Data Scientists (24) lead the pack.

Environmental Scientists (18), Designers (16), and Marketers (14) keep us grounded.

From Public Policy to Agriculture to Journalism — every voice matters.

Python Knowledge

In the age of data, how well do we speak Python?

35 Beginners (23%) — print("hello world") never felt so good.

55 Intermediates (37%) — the largest group!

40 Advanced (27%) and 20 Experts (13%) — together 77% are Intermediate or above.

Favorite SDGs

We asked: “Which SDG is closest to your heart?”

SDG 13 — Climate Action won with 32 votes.

Quality Education (22) and Clean Water (18) round out the top 3.

Our passions span the entire 2030 Agenda.

What Fuels Us?

What do we drink every week?

2,100 liters of water — enough to fill 10 bathtubs!

525 liters of coffee — 3.5 liters per person, roughly 5 cups a day.

Plus 300L tea, 150L soda, 120L juice, and 45L energy drinks.

Fun fact: Our weekly water bottles lined up would stretch 2.1 km — 20 football fields!

How Do We Move?

Every week we cover 15,000 km combined — a third of the way around Earth!

Cars lead at 6,750 km/week (45%).

Buses at 4,500 km (30%).

2,250 km walked and 1,500 km biked — 25 km zero-emission per person.

Challenge: Switch car trips under 5 km to biking and save 820 kg CO2/week!

Three Words About the SDGs

“Name three words when you think about the SDGs.”

Future, Hope, and Action dominated.

Together, Planet, and Change — a shared journey toward transformation.

Innovation and Community — solutions from collective creativity.

The Great Plant Census

“How many houseplants have you accidentally composted?”

Only 22 (15%) claim zero casualties.

45 people lost 1-3 plants — the biggest group.

38 lost 4-7, 25 confessed to 8+, and 20 said “lost count.” Total: ~487 plants.

That’s us. 150 people. 50 teams.

From coffee to kilometers, from SDGs to fallen houseplants — every data point tells our story.

Now let’s get to work — the future won’t build itself.

(…but at least we have 525 liters of coffee to help.)
